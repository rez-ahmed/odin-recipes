One-Skillet Mexican Quinoa

Description

One-Skillet Mexican Quinoa is a vibrant and nutritious meal packed with bold flavors and wholesome ingredients—all made in just one pan. This easy-to-make dish combines protein-rich quinoa, black beans, corn, tomatoes, and a mix of Mexican spices for a hearty and satisfying experience.

With zesty lime, fresh cilantro, and optional toppings like avocado or shredded cheese, this recipe is perfect for busy weeknights or meal prepping. It's gluten-free, vegetarian, and full of flavor, making it a favorite for both health-conscious eaters and lovers of Mexican cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium jalapeño pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 (15 ounce) can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (14.5 ounce) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup yellow corn
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon red pepper flakes, or to taste
  • 1 ½ teaspoons chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • kosher salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 medium avocado - peeled, pitted, and diced
  • 1 medium lime, juiced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sauté jalapeño pepper and garlic in the hot oil until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Stir black beans, tomatoes, corn, quinoa, and chicken broth into the skillet. Season with pepper flakes, chili powder, cumin, salt, and black pepper; bring to a boil.
  3. Cover the skillet with a lid, reduce heat to low, and simmer until quinoa is tender and liquid is mostly absorbed, about 20 minutes.
  4. Add avocado, lime juice, and cilantro; stir until combined.
